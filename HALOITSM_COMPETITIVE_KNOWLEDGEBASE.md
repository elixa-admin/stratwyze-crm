**Objection 1: "ServiceNow is the industry standard"**
- **Root cause**: Market leader position, installed base, analyst recognition
- **Why they say it**: Brand perception, competitor ecosystem, "safe choice" bias
- **HaloITSM Counter**:
  - "Industry standard doesn't mean best fit for YOUR use case. Standard = slow, expensive, overcomplicated."
  - "Gartner recognizes HaloITSM as leader for organizations seeking speed + cost efficiency."
  - "Standard = lock-in. You're paying for modules you don't need, customizations you inherited, and services you can't stop."
  - **Positioning**: "We respect ServiceNow's market position. Here's why HaloITSM is the better choice FOR YOU:
    - Your budget is constrained? HaloITSM is 70% cheaper.
    - Your timeline is tight? HaloITSM is 50% faster.
    - Your team is struggling with adoption? HaloITSM's UX wins.
    - You want to focus on IT ops, not managing a platform? HaloITSM's cloud-native approach."

**Objection 2: "We have 12+ years of ServiceNow customizations—switching is too risky"**
- **Root cause**: Sunk cost fallacy, switching cost perception, "the devil you know"
- **Why they say it**: Real pain of dealing with legacy customizations, but also fear of change
- **HaloITSM Counter**:
  - "I understand the concern. Here's the truth: those 12 years of customizations are your liability, not your asset."
  - "Why? Because every ServiceNow upgrade breaks custom code. You're perpetually fixing broken workflows."
  - "HaloITSM migration moves your clean data, not your broken customizations. You start with a modern platform."
  - "Migration risk is lower than staying: you can't add features without breaking SN, you can't upgrade without risk. HaloITSM removes that risk."
  - "Timeline: Typical SN-to-HaloITSM migration = 4-6 months. Your data moves cleanly. Workflows are rebuilt modern."
  - **Positioning**: "Your ServiceNow system is like maintaining a 20-year-old car. Yes, you've invested heavily, but the cost of maintenance is exploding. HaloITSM is the new car—reliable, efficient, no tech debt."

**Objection 3: "Our team is trained on ServiceNow—retraining is expensive"**
- **Root cause**: Switching cost, team productivity loss during transition
- **Why they say it**: Valid concern about disruption
- **HaloITSM Counter**:
  - "HaloITSM's interface is 10x more intuitive. Your team will be productive faster in HaloITSM than they are in SN."
  - "ServiceNow requires 2-3 months to reach productivity (developer mindset). HaloITSM requires 2 weeks (IT ops mindset)."
  - "You're not retraining on complexity—you're retraining on simplicity. Your team will thank you."
  - "Training costs are lower: HaloITSM requires 40 hours per user vs. SN's 120+ hours."
  - **Positioning**: "Yes, your team knows ServiceNow. But knowing a complex system that frustrates them daily is not a competitive advantage. HaloITSM is easier, faster, and your team will be MORE productive, not less."

**Objection 4: "ServiceNow has [specific feature] we depend on"**
- **Root cause**: Feature parity concern, workflow dependency
- **Why they say it**: Legitimate feature gap concern
- **HaloITSM Counter**:
  - "What specific feature are you concerned about?"
