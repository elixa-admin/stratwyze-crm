The Core Decision Threshold

$5K threshold: This is the line between autonomy and oversight. Agents/directors execute anything that costs less than $5K without asking you. Anything $5K+ requires director or your approval, depending on scale. This keeps you in the loop on material decisions without micromanaging.
Why $5K? At a startup, $5K is meaningful but not board-level. It covers most operational decisions (tools, deployments, travel, small hires). Forces escalation for things that matter, but doesn't bottleneck the org.

Note: Directors are not human—they're AI agents with role specialization. They decide fast (<5 min SLA) because they're not context-limited like humans. But they escalate strategic decisions (Tier 3+) to you because those require human judgment.

Escalation Flow

1

Task Arrives (Any Agent)

An agent detects a decision point: "Should we deploy?" "Should we buy this tool?" "Should we pivot?"

2

Assess Cost / Impact

Agent estimates: "$2K deploy cost" or "$30K annual tool" or "$500K product rebrand"

3

Route Based on Tier

<$5K? Agent auto-executes (post to dashboard). $5K–$50K? Route to relevant director. >$50K? Route to you.

4

Decision Made (SLA)

Tier 1: Immediate. Tier 2: <5 min (director decides). Tier 3: <30 min (you decide with input). Tier 4: <2 hours (board + you).

5

Execute & Log

Decision executed, logged in audit trail with approval chain. Dashboard updated.

Special Cases & Exceptions

Security Issues (Auto-Escalate)

ANY security issue escalates immediately to Anderson, regardless of cost. If critical → you're alerted. Critical = data breach, account compromise, vulnerability in prod. Process: Detect → isolate → escalate → patch → post-mortem.

Customer Escalations (Human Loop)

Customer complaint or churn event → Cypher handles. If customer is at risk of leaving → Persephone decides. If contract at risk → you're involved.

Tie-Breaking (Director Consensus)

If two directors disagree on a decision in their overlapping area (e.g., Niobe wants feature that costs more than Dozer's budget), they escalate to you for tie-break. SLA: <10 min.

Legal / Compliance Issues (Auto-Escalate)

Any legal question or compliance question → you're looped in immediately. The Architect tracks (GDPR, taxes, contracts), but you make final call.

Emergency / Critical Incident (Bypass Authority)

System down, data loss, severe security breach → agents execute emergency response without waiting for approvals. You're notified asynchronously. Example: Tank rolls back a bad deploy, logs it, tells you after it's fixed.

Parallel Execution (Don't Block)

If multiple agents/directors are waiting on a decision, don't block others. Example: You're deciding on a $100K tool, but dev team keeps shipping features anyway (Tier 1). Don't cascade the wait.

Important Notes & Guardrails

⚠️ No Threshold Abuse: Directors can't game the system by breaking a $10K decision into two $5K decisions. If the intent is one decision, treat it as one decision.
⚠️ Escalation Fatigue: If you're approving >10 decisions per day, thresholds are too aggressive. Raise them. If <1 decision per week, they're too loose. Lower them.
📌 Regular Review: Every Sprint 4, review authority logs. Are decisions being made fast enough? Are escalations happening at the right level? Adjust thresholds as needed.
📌 Override Authority: You can override any director decision, but document why. This teaches the org and prevents future escalations on the same issue.
